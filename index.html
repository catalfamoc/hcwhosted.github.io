Cohorted Voluntary Self Quarantine

This guideline applies to frontline healthcare workers (doctors, nurses, med techs, respiratory therapists, radiology techs, and others) who are in front-line emergency medicine roles, working and living in direct contact with COVID-19 patients, and are planning to self-quarantine for the safety of their loved ones.

About

Cohorted Voluntary Self Quarantine (CVSQ)

This is the page meant to provide you with additional information about the CVSQ initiative in several key areas.

Mission Statement

Website mission

  1. Enroll interested Health Care Workers (HCW) in CVSQ
  2. Provide information on this community-driven effort

Initiative's effort

  • To offer physical and psychosocial support to healthcare workers (HCWs) and protection for their families and communities by providing dedicated HCW housing for HCWs in cities implementing CVSQ throughout the COVID-19 pandemic surge.
  • Main service
    • Quarantined housing (separate areas for 1) asymptomatic and working 2) symptomatic with testing pending, 3) +Covid19 not working, and 4) Post-tour of duty quarantine awaiting re-entry into home
  • Secondary service
    • Instrumental support (food, laundry, etc) Social support, pysch support, monitoring (epi surveillance)

Why is CVSQ housing important?

  • The ability to protect physicians and their families is a big factor in the resilience of the healthcare system. Cohorted volunteer self quarantine (CVSQ) can reduce transmission in the Healthcare Workforce (HCW) families.
  • Psychosocial support in CVSQ may also reduce trauma and PTSD in the HCW and among their families.
  • The way the COVID-19 outbreak was ended in China was by isolating healthcare workers.
